## 🔊 HVAC_Waveform_Model.pdf

### Summary

Self‑directed physics and engineering project modeling outdoor HVAC sound propagation using wave‑based acoustics, with selective AI assistance used for derivation checking and structural clarity during paper development.

### Key Points

- Developed a frequency‑domain acoustic model for single‑unit, line‑array, and square‑array HVAC configurations.

- Derived behavior for Fresnel zones, constructive/destructive interference, and ground‑reflection phase interactions.

- Produced a structured technical paper detailing the theoretical framework and mapping each component to future Python implementation, incorporating AI support for organization, clarity, and verification of mathematical steps.

- Designed the model to be modular for future simulation or program‑evaluation workflows.

### My Role

- Sole author of all derivations, modeling decisions, and technical documentation.

- Built the mathematical framework and engineering assumptions from scratch.

- Used AI as a collaborative drafting tool: I prompted, guided, corrected, pushed back, and validated the work — including checking sections with multiple AIs — but I did not write the paper myself.

## 📘 Concepts_of_Assessment.pdf

**Reeves, T. D., Onder, Y., & Kraner, C. (2023).** *Validation and invariance of the Conceptions of Assessment‑III Abridged (COA‑IIIA) among pre‑service and in‑service teachers in the United States.* **Educational Assessment, Evaluation and Accountability, 35**(3), 419–447. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11092-023-09407-4

### Summary

A full psychometric evaluation of the Conceptions of Assessment III‑Abridged (COA‑IIIA) using U.S. pre‑service and in‑service teacher samples. The study tested previously published COA‑IIIA factor structures, identified the best‑fitting model for U.S. teachers, and examined measurement invariance and reliability across groups.

### Key Points

- Tested 16 prior COA‑IIIA models using CFA.  
- Identified best‑fitting structure (Model 6c).  
- Demonstrated metric invariance across teacher groups.  
- Provided reliability evidence for retained factors.

### My Role

- Conducted all statistical analyses (CFA, invariance, reliability).  
- Wrote the technical analysis documents used to build the published manuscript.  
- Generated model comparisons and fit diagnostics that shaped the final paper.

## 👓 Google_Glass_Integration

**Tscholl, M., Demir, F., & Kraner, C. (2022).** *Google‑based Learning Analytics System for Assessment (GLASA): Design considerations and classroom implementation.* In **Proceedings of the 22nd IEEE International Conference on Advanced Learning Technologies (ICALT 2022)** (pp. 245–249). IEEE.

### Summary

Prototype system using Google Glass to deliver real‑time, private feedback during student presentations.

### Key Points

- Provided instant cues (e.g., *Speed up*, *Eye contact*, *Louder*).  
- Students reported high usability and increased self‑efficacy.  
- Identified design improvements for future iterations.

### My Role

- Technical onboarding: learned the device and created usable workflows.  
- Instructional enablement: taught participants how to use the Glass system.  
- System integration: ensured the device and interface were functional for study deployment.  
- Did not conduct interviews or sentiment analysis; authorship reflects technical contributions.
